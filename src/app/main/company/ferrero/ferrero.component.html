In the 1940s, Pietro Ferrero transformed a pastry shop into a factory. The Ferro Family was the first Italian manufacturer after World War 2 to open production sites and offices abroad in the confectionary sector, which brought about making the company into an international group.

In 1946, Pietro Ferrero a confectioner and small-time pastry maker added hazelnut as a way to save money on chocolate. This innovation led to the development of Nutella. Today, The Ferrero Group is considered the 3rd largest chocolate producer and confectionery company in the world today. Their global headquarters is based in Luxembourg, Italy and they are present in 55 countries and territories around the world: 25 production plants in 5 continents, 8 Ferrero Hazelnut Company manufacturing plants, 6 agricultural companies and the Michele Ferrero Entrepreneurial Project, active in Cameroon, South Africa and India.

Among the chocolate confectionery segment, Ferrero has a 13.5% share of the global market, ranking it the 3rd largest player in 2017/2018. In the Sweet packaged foods segment, Ferrero Group has a 3.4% share of the global market, making it the third largest player in 2018. In 2017/2018, Ferrero sold €10.7 billion worth of packaged confectionery goods.
